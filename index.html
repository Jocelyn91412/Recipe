Lemon New York-Style Cheesecake

A Little Background Info

Cheesecake has a long history all the way from ancient Greece. It was invented in 776 B.C. and has change a lot since then. The very first Cheesecake was made from cheese, wheat flour, and honey. They were served to athletes as a source of energy. Cheesecake in ancient Greece looked more like a loaf than what it looks like now. In the 1920s a New York restaurateur Arnold Reuben replaced the cheese with cream cheese giving it its iconic smooth texture and look.

Less Talking More Cooking

Stoof You Need

The Cheesecake Itself

  • Creamcheese 150g
  • Yogurt
  • Heavy Whipping Cream
  • Sugar
  • Digestive Biscuits(Any other kinds of crakers work too)
  • Butter
  • Lemons
  • Gelatin
  • HOT Water
  • A Container That You Think Will Fit

The Lemon Curd

  • Lemon Juice
  • Lemon Zest
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Egg Yokes
  • Butter

TIME TO MAKE IT

  1. Smash the buscuit really really small. A ziplock bag and a water bottle will work just fine:D
  2. Melt the butter. If you are lazy like me, microwave it in a glass container for about 30 seconds.
  3. Now mix the crushed buscuits into the melted butter
  4. Put the mixture into your container and spread it evenly at the bottom
  5. Use the bottom of a cup or a spoon to compress and flatten it. Use what ever works for you, I used a measuring cup once because I couldn't find anything else.
  6. In a new bowl, container, whatever you use to mix the stuff in. Add creamcheese, sugar, yogurt, and heavy whipping cream. DO NOT WHIP YOUR WHIPPING CREAM
  7. Mix it all together until smooth
  8. Opitonal* add lemon juice and lemon zest into the filling. There are no right or wring measurements, just add more until you are satisfied with the taste. It won't kill you to try the filling, everything in there is safe to eat without heating.
  9. Add how water to the gelatin in another container and mix together. Do this step quickly or else it will solidify too fast
  10. Then put the gelatine mixture into the filling and mix them all together.
  11. put the filling inte container on top of the base and set it in the fridge to chill. Since we dont want the lemon curd to mix with the cheesecake, let it solidify first.

While the cheesecake is in the fridge, you can start making the lemon curd

  1. In a double boiler or small pot, add lemon juice, lemon zest, sugar, salt, and egg yokes.
  2. Mix it all together and heat on low heat and whisk until the mixture thickens. This can take a REALLY long time. Just be patient.
  3. As it thickens, you can taste it and add more lemon juice, sugar, or salt.
  4. Once it thickens, remove from heat and add cold butter.

FINALLY AFTER WAITING FOR A REALL REALLY LONG TIME... put the lemon curd on top of the cheesecake put it in the fridge...AND WAIT SOME MORE

NOW you can eat your wonderful, amazing, awesome, yummylicious cheesecake
